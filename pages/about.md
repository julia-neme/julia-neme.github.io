I was born in Buenos Aires, Argentina and have been drawn to the ocean for as long as I can remember. I was fortunate enough to be able to sail with my family quite frequently, and practise different water sports since I was very young. My favourite was, and remains, windsurfing. 

<img align="right" height="170" src="/assets/img/CVphoto.jpg">

This love for the ocean, combined with a natural aptitude for maths and physics, made my decision to study physical oceanography quite easy. I studied at the University of Buenos Aires, at the Atmospheric and Ocean Sciences Department (DCAO). I will be forever grateful for the love and dedication to science that I found in everyone there. They were 6 very happy years. 

There was something though that left me somewhat unsatisfied: I really wanted to do Antarctic-related research. And I also wanted to live overseas. So I found myself applying, and obtaining, a scholarship to pursue a PhD degree at the University of New South Wales, in Sydney (Australia) with the possibility to study the ocean around Antarctica in the context of climate change alongside an amazing team of researchers. So this is where I'm currently at, studying the variability of the Weddell Gyre in connection to possible driving mechanisms and timescales.
